The Rise of Health Food in Los Angeles: By Emilee Bick

The citizens of Los Angeles are rising in a new age of health craze. Resturaunts, shops, and concepts are being revealed all around the city. There are crazy ideas, ingredients, and mixtures that are being invented everyday and introduced to a wide variety of people. I wanted to showcase a few of these foods, drinks, and snacks, that have unique health benefits.

Matcha Lattes

Matcha has its origin in Japanese culture when they usually served it with hot water and the ground up leaf. Although matcha has a new relevance in the coffee and tea world as being a new ingredient used in lattes, also known as being served with foamed milk. Matcha is rich in antioxidants and detoxifies the body.

Chia Seeds

Chia seeds have been used in many culinary ways. The most common are puddings and drinks because they contain alot of protein but also abbsorb water very easily so they tend to expand quickly. They retain a lot of liquid so they provide hydration when you eat or drink something with them. They are very small but they contain fibers, omega 3 fatty acids, and protein. They have also been known to help lose body fat, and now a days everyone is looking for a method to help lose weight easily.

Coconuts

Coconuts have only become very popular in the healthfood world recently. Coconut milk, water, oil, and meat have been on the rise. Coconuts are rich in electrolytes, aid hydration and act like a probiotic to the immune system, supplying good bacteria to the digestive system.

Kale

Kale is a leafy green that has been brought into the health world in a whole new way. This fiberous leaf is said to be the one of the most nutrient dense foods on the planet. There is a abundance of good vitamins in the leaf, including rare antioxidants. Kale has also been linked to cancer prevention, cholestrol balancing, and weight loss. This sour and tangy leaf is used in juices, and almost started the era of juicing and the health benefits of juice. It can also be cooked down with seasoning, or even made into chips! This versatile leaf has started a revolution of health foods.
